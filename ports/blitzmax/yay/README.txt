This is a simple 5-die "poker dice" style game.
You roll the dice 3 times per round and claim a "score", with the aim to get a high score at the end.

You can change the colour of the backdrop between Green, Red and Blue, in that order, by clicking the Backdrop button.
Exit game does exactly as expected. You can also close the game by pressing Escape, or clicking the X in the corner of the window.

HOW TO PLAY - the short version
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Click "New Game" button to start.
The dice will roll. Click on a die to "trash" that die when you roll. When a die will be trashed, a trashcan icon will be shown. You can click it again to keep that die.

After the initial roll, you get to roll two more times either all, or just "trashed" dice.
Once you're out of rolls, or you're happy with an achievable score, click on a score to lock it in.
The dice will roll again, and the process will repeat.
If you don't have a scoring hand, you'll have to pick a non-scoring value, and gain no points in the process, so choose carefully what to sacrifice!
Once all scores are locked, the game ends.

Try and score as high as possible!

ABOUT THE GAME - in more detail
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The aim of the game is to score as many points as possible using the five dice.
You can roll the dice two times per turn, after the initial roll.
Once you have rolled three times, or there's a score you're happy with, you must choose a scoring method.
When you choose to lock in your score, that scoring type is removed from play.
If after your three rolls you have no scoring combination, or a scoring combination that is already locked in, you can select any other non-locked scoring method, however you will score 0 for that selection.
The game ends when all scoring types are removed from play.

Points are determined based on two tiers and are detailed below.

The first tier is scoring based on the value of each die -
Ones (scores 1-6)
Twos (scores 2,4,6,8,10,12)
Threes (scores 3,6,9,12,15,18)
Fours (scores 4,8,12,16,20)
Fives (scores 5,10,15,20,25)
Sixes (scores 6,12,18,24,30)
For example, rolling a hand with four threes can use the "Threes" score for 12 points - it could also apply to the "Four of a Kind" score for the same .

For every maximum scoring hand (5,10,15,20,25,30) in the first tier you will gain an additional 20 points. Scoring *all* maximums in this tier doubles the bonus from 120 to 240.

The second tier is similar to Poker hands, and are scored based on the die values.

One Pair - Uses the highest value single pair for scoring. Potential scores are 2,4,6,8,10,12.
Two Pairs - Scores based on the values of the two pairs you have. the fifth die is not counted. Potential scores are 4 to 24.
Three of a Kind - Scores based on the value of a three of a kind in your hand. The other two die are not counted, even if they are a pair. Potential scores are 3,6,9,12,15,18.
Four of a Kind - Score based on the die values of a Four of a Kind - Potential scores are 4,8,12,16,20,24.
Low Straight - You have a hand of 1 2 3 4 5 in any order. Scores 15.
High Straight - You have a hand of 2 3 4 5 6 in any order. Scores 20.
Full House - Your hand contains a pair, and a Three of a Kind. Potential scores range from 5-30.
Chance - Adds the values of all dice for your score. Scores range from 5-30.
Flush - All die must be the same value. Scores a fixed amount of 50. If all sixes, scores 100.
